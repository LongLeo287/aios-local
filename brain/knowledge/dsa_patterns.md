# 🧩 Top DSA Patterns for AI Agents

## Description
A technical guide to the most effective Data Structures and Algorithms (DSA) patterns for AI-driven problem-solving, enabling agents to recognize and apply optimal computational strategies.

## 1. Sliding Window
- **When to use:** Arrays or strings where you need to find a sub-segment (contiguous).
- **Goal:** Reduce $O(n^2)$ to $O(n)$.
- **Example:** Longest substring with K unique characters.

## 2. Two Pointers
- **When to use:** Sorted arrays or linked lists.
- **Goal:** Compare or move from ends/middle to solve in $O(n)$.
- **Example:** Three sum, Palindrome check.

## 3. Fast & Slow Pointers (Tortoise and Hare)
- **When to use:** Cyclic data structures (Linked Lists).
- **Example:** Cycle detection.

## 4. BFS / DFS (Graph & Trees)
- **BFS (Breadth-First):** Shortest path in unweighted graphs.
- **DFS (Depth-First):** Exhaustive search, backtracking.

## 5. Binary Search
- **When to use:** Searching in sorted data or searching for an optimal answer in a range.
- **Goal:** $O(\log n)$ performance.

## 6. Dynamic Programming (DP)
- **When to use:** Overlapping subproblems + Optimal substructure.
- **Strategy:** Bottom-up (Tabulation) or Top-down (Memoization).

## 7. Backtracking
- **When to use:** Finding all (or some) solutions that satisfy constraints.
- **Example:** N-Queens, Sudoku solver.

## 8. Greedy
- **When to use:** Local optimal choice leads to global optimal.
- **Example:** Activity selection, Huffman coding.
